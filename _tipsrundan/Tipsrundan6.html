Tipsrundan #6

Welcome to the first number of Tipsrundan 2020! We hope everyone had a great vacation and are back ready for some action.

[PINNED] Coursers, Conferences & more

We hope you all know but for the newcomers & people who forgot during the vacation: with your own initiative and a quick talk to your manager you can take certificates, courses & go to conferences.

We have a pinned post on the internal Slack (if you're not invited ask for an invitation!).

Refresh your memory: Competence Groups

We have a few Competence Groups, with room for more, that you can follow and join in on.


Machine Learning

The Machine Learning group is run by Hampus Londögård. The focus this far has been on NLP (text) but might change soon(ish) and doing the implementation (rather than using finished API:s). Moving forward we'll try to find things to help out with at Malmö Office, so if you know any tedious manual text-related task please reach out and we'll see if we can help out!


Algorithms

The Algorithm group is run by Kasper Bratz and is really awesome. You'll often find yourself competing with peers on how to optimally solve a problem. This can be everything from landing a rocket on mars, winning a Bombeman competition to capture the flag on websites.
EXTRA: There's a need for a fill-in for Kasper while he has time off (tjänsteledigt). Reach out to Kasper or Hampus if you want to keep the group running while he's gone!


Front-End

The Front-End group is run by Carlos Velasco and focuses on UX mainly. No framework nor any language is locked in (but javascript has been the focus this far).


Cloud

In the Cloud Competence group the current focus is mostly on Azure Certification, reach out to your closest manager (or Nima Goniband) to find out more!


New groups

A competence group can be everything from a continual group that meets once a month to a temporary study group where you grab a pizza and try to complete an online course over the course of a few weeks (billing AFRY for the pizza, course & time ;)).
If you want to create a new group just reach out to us (Hampus Londögård & Hassan Ftouni or your closest manager!

New year, new me - find yourself a fun project

For the non-programmer who wants to learn

For everyone who can't code today there's a big bonus in learning it, you can complete a lot of annoying tasks much faster using a "simple" scripting language such as Python. Be it something with nubmers or perhaps users, you can take the file and parse it really easy using Python.
Here's some projects to get yourself started with some tooling.

And here's a guide and cheatsheet and another.


For the programmer

Austin Z. Henley crafted a really nice list of challenging projects here.

There's a little for everyone, from games to OS to Database from scratch creation! Make sure to not miss out the list at the end that got aggregated from the Hacker News & Reddit discussions.


For the ones who really don't want to code

Life is not just about code, find that one thing that you want to do and make sure to spend time on it this year, whether it be that thing you always wanted to build or place you wanted to go. Make sure to make 2020 the best year possible! :)

The business card that run linux

In an earlier newsletter we show-cased how a company could squeeze SQLite into becoming a beast (serving > 4 million queries / second) - they took it one step further than normally.

Here's another one who did just the same, he took a business card and made it into something that can run linux. It's amazing and looks, in my opinion, really cool.

Make sure to read Georges' story on how he did it with a good cup of coffee (or any other drink really), I'm sure this can please more people than just the ones into embedded devices!
